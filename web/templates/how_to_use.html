Running the Test

1. Preparation

Before running the test:

  • Ensure you're well-rested and recovered
  • Have a light meal 2-3 hours before
  • Stay hydrated
  • Choose a flat route or track
  • Make sure your heart rate monitor is working properly

2. The Test

During the test:

  • Start with a 10-15 minute warm-up
  • Choose a target heart rate that feels moderate (you should be able to hold a conversation)
  • Run for 45-60 minutes at this constant heart rate
  • Record your entire workout using your GPS watch or fitness app

3. Analyzing the Results

After the test:

  • Export your workout file (.fit or .gpx format)
  • Upload the file to this analyzer
  • Use the sliders to select the test portion (excluding warm-up)
  • Click "Analyze Sections" to see your results

Tips for Success

  • Choose a day with moderate weather conditions
  • Avoid running the test if you're feeling under the weather
  • Don't eat or drink during the test (except water)
  • Focus on maintaining a steady heart rate
  • Ignore your pace - let it vary as needed to maintain the heart rate

Understanding the Results

The analyzer will show you:

  • Your average heart rate and pace for each section
  • The change in your pace-to-heart rate ratio
  • Whether you were above, at, or below your aerobic threshold
  • Recommendations for your next test or training

If your pace-to-heart rate ratio increased by:

  • Less than 3.5%: Your target heart rate was below your aerobic threshold. Try again with a higher heart rate.
  • 3.5-5%: You've found your aerobic threshold! Use this heart rate for your easy runs.
  • More than 5%: Your target heart rate was above your aerobic threshold. Try again with a lower heart rate.
