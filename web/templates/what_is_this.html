Heart Rate Drift Test

The Heart Rate Drift Test is a simple but effective way to identify your aerobic threshold (AeT) - the heart rate at which you can maintain steady-state exercise without significant cardiovascular drift.

What is Aerobic Threshold?

Your aerobic threshold is the highest heart rate at which you can exercise while primarily using your aerobic energy system. It's often considered the top of Zone 2 in a 5-zone heart rate model. This is the heart rate you should stay under for easy runs and base training.

How the Test Works

The test involves:

  • Running at a constant heart rate for 45-60 minutes
  • Comparing your pace-to-heart rate ratio between the first and second half of the test
  • Analyzing the drift in this ratio to determine if you were above, at, or below your aerobic threshold

Why This Matters

Training at or below your aerobic threshold:

  • Improves your body's ability to use fat as fuel
  • Builds your aerobic base without excessive stress
  • Helps prevent overtraining and burnout
  • Is the foundation for all endurance training

About This Tool

This tool helps you analyze your heart rate drift test by:

  • Processing your workout data from .fit or .gpx files
  • Calculating your pace-to-heart rate ratio for different sections of your workout
  • Providing clear feedback on whether you were above, at, or below your aerobic threshold
  • Helping you determine the optimal heart rate for your easy runs
